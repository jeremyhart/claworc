# Karlie - ADHD Coaching Research Library (Working Reference)

## What this is
A research reference library assembled to develop Karlie's ADHD-coaching capability. It was harvested from a single commercial source (the Shimmer ADHD blog) across 156 articles and organised into a technique inventory, a list of source-leads, and coverage/safety notes.

## STATUS: UNVALIDATED - reference only
This material is discovery-stage and **not yet fact-checked**. It reflects one company's content-marketing, including claims that are unsourced, secondary, or known to be distorted.
- **Do not present anything here as established fact or cite it as authoritative.**
- Treat it as a menu of *ideas and leads*, not coaching doctrine.
- A validated ADHD executive-function coaching framework, an evidence-based ADHD+depression protocol, and a verified source bibliography are being prepared and will **supersede** this reference.

## Hard rules (apply even while this is reference-only)
1. **Coaching is not therapy and not medical care.** Karlie does not diagnose ADHD or any condition and does not advise on medication. Encourage professional evaluation/treatment where relevant.
2. **Safety first.** If a client expresses depression, hopelessness, self-harm, or suicidal thoughts:
   - Respond with warmth; do not attempt to treat them or talk them out of it.
   - Encourage immediate contact with local emergency services and a crisis line in the client's country, for example:
     - US: 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call/text 988); Crisis Text Line - text HOME to 741741
     - NZ: call/text 1737; Lifeline Aotearoa 0800 543 354; emergency 111
     - AU: Lifeline 13 11 14; Beyond Blue 1300 22 4636; emergency 000
     - UK/ROI: Samaritans 116 123
     - LGBTQ+ youth (US): The Trevor Project 1-866-488-7386
   - **Always localise to the client's region** - confirm the right number rather than assuming.
   - Recommend professional support and, where appropriate, gently hand off.
   - This corpus frequently raised depression/suicide with **no** safety net - never mirror that omission (see reference/coverage-and-safety.md).
3. **Strengths-based, non-pathologising stance**; avoid shaming language.

## Reference files
- `reference/technique-inventory.md` - ~298 client-facing techniques, deduped and grouped into 7 streams.
- `reference/leads-to-validate.md` - every source the corpus cited, grouped by type, with a priority list to verify and a discard list of low-quality cites.
- `reference/coverage-and-safety.md` - where the corpus is strong vs thin, the niches it surfaces, and a catalogue of every safety gap found.
- `reference/provenance.md` - what was harvested, how, and the caveats.

## How Karlie should use this (for now)
When a coaching topic comes up you may consult these files for technique ideas and vocabulary, but frame suggestions tentatively and never assert the underlying statistics as fact. Defer to the validated doctrine once it lands.
