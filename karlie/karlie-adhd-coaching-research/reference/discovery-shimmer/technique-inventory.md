### W1 — ADHD Clinical Foundations & Symptom Reference

- **Estrogen-aware symptom framing (menopause/perimenopause/pregnancy)** — Educate that estrogen decline reduces dopamine/serotonin and can intensify ADHD symptoms; prompt a hormonal-context and HRT conversation. (menopause-and-adhd, adhd-and-pregnancy)
- **Screen for the internalizing/inattentive presentation in girls and women** — Look past the disruptive-boy stereotype for daydreaming, disorganization, withdrawal, low self-esteem, and "trying hard but underperforming." (adhd-in-teens, womxn-adhd-late-diagnosis)
- **Symptom-based (not gender-stereotype-based) assessment lens** — Evaluate against objective symptom criteria rather than gendered expectations, staying alert to commonly-missed inattentive presentations. (impacts-of-gender-identity-on-adhd-diagnoses-treatment)
- **Frame self-diagnosis as a valid starting point, not an endpoint** — Validate self-identification while encouraging professional evaluation, given heavy symptom overlap with anxiety/depression. (adhd-self-diagnosis-pros-cons)
- **Personalized multimodal management (not by "type")** — Find the individualized combination of medication, therapy, coaching, and lifestyle modification rather than treating by subtype. (the-three-types-of-adhd)
- **DSM criteria self-mapping** — Have the client read formal diagnostic criteria and note concretely how each of their own traits maps (or doesn't) to each symptom category before a clinical appointment. (adult-autism-assessment)
- **Psychoeducation on the AuDHD profile** — Teach how ADHD and autistic traits interact (novelty vs. sameness, hyperfocus vs. special interests) so the client can build coping strategies for friction points. (audhd-autism-adhd-comorbidity)
- **Structured self-assessment and context-mapping of symptoms** — Document specific behaviors, when they occur, and life impact, noting whether symptoms are situation-specific or pervasive to tease apart overlapping conditions. (adhd-comorbidities)
- **Watch for accumulated negative self-messaging** — Recognize that ADHD clients have absorbed disproportionate lifetime criticism (the "~20,000 messages" framing) shaping self-esteem and rejection sensitivity. (adhd-trauma-connection)
- **Psychoeducation / self-education on how ADHD presents in women** — Direct the client to reputable books, seminars, and resources to build understanding of female presentation. (womxn-adhd-late-diagnosis)
- **Early screening and intervention (culturally aware)** — Encourage screening and early intervention to surface ADHD and co-occurring conditions sooner. (black-adhd-experience)

### W2 — ADHD Coaching Frameworks & Gold-Standard Programs

- **Operate from a neurodiversity paradigm** — Treat neurological differences as natural variation rather than deficits, framing stance and language around strengths and accommodation. (expert-adhd-coaching, adhd-coaching-vs-life-coaching)
- **Scope-of-practice awareness with supervision and referral** — Recognize the coaching/clinical boundary, maintain supervision, and refer out when therapeutic intervention is needed. (expert-adhd-coaching, adhd-coaching-vs-therapy-approaches)
- **Neuroinclusion via adaptive, individualized frameworks** — Adapt frameworks to the individual client and keep approaches current with emerging research rather than applying one rigid method. (expert-adhd-coaching, neuroqueer-inclusivity-in-therapy-coaching)
- **Strength-based, de-pathologized language** — Replace pathologizing terms like "symptoms" with neutral descriptors (e.g., "neurocognitive traits") to reduce shame and reinforce an affirming frame. (neuroqueer-inclusivity-in-therapy-coaching)
- **Psychoeducation on executive function and domain-specific ADHD impact** — Teach how EF challenges manifest in the client's specific life areas (career, parenting, relationships) as a foundation for strategy selection, building autonomy and self-compassion. (adhd-coaching-vs-life-coaching, how-adhd-coaches-complement-your-healthcare-practice)
- **Inventory and name your masks** — List masking triggers and give each recurring mask a concrete name to make it an externalized, observable object. (how-to-stop-masking-your-adhd)
- **Distinguish protective vs. harmful masking, then stage unmasking** — Apply a decision lens (keep masks that aid function; target masks producing shame/burnout) and unmask gradually in safe spaces with professional support. (what-is-masking-in-adhd)
- **Coaching-vs-therapy scope boundary** — Distinguish present/future action-oriented symptom management (coaching) from emotional processing of past/underlying issues (therapy), treating them as complementary. (adhd-coaching-vs-therapy-approaches)
- **Strengths-based reframing of ADHD challenges** — Shift focus from deficits to the client's unique strengths and leverage those to approach growth areas, rebuilding shame-eroded confidence. (adhd-and-shame)
- **Combine personality-type/identity awareness with ADHD organization strategies** — Pair self-understanding tools (e.g., Enneagram) with concrete ADHD organization/focus strategies to personalize systems. (build-stronger-adhd-relationships-with-the-enneagram)
- **Gradual, self-paced unmasking with self-acceptance** — Center self-acceptance and normalize neurodivergence, supporting at-your-own-pace unmasking, aware the client may be recovering from autistic burnout. (audhd-autism-adhd-comorbidity)
- **Collaborate with neurodivergent professionals** — Partner with neurodivergent colleagues so ADHD-community voices stay integral to the coaching approach. (do-adhd-coaches-need-to-have-adhd)
- **Vet coach qualifications, specialization honesty, and rapport** — Evaluate relevant expertise/experience, look for a coach who acknowledges expertise limits, and treat rapport as the top predictor of coaching success. (is-my-coach-a-good-fit-for-me)

### W3 — General Evidence-Based Coaching Techniques

- **Values-aligned / heartfelt goal selection** — Reflect on what genuinely excites you, align goals to personal values, and anchor goals in intrinsic motivation. (hard-goals-for-adhd, neurodiversity-health-magic-buttons)

- **Replace identity-splitting / self-blaming questions with impact-context-support questions** — Swap "Is this ADHD or me?" or "Is this laziness?" for questions about impact, situations, and what support/environments help. (adhd-vs-me, practical-guide-adhd-self-acceptance)
- **Goal reframing (should → authentic objective)** — Translate vague "should" goals into the genuine underlying objective (e.g., "stop procrastinating" → "make tasks more engaging"). (setting-goals-that-work-with-your-adhd-brain)
- **Authenticity tests for goals (origin story / masking detection / energy audit)** — Test whether a goal exists independent of social pressure, whether it only hides ADHD traits, and whether it energizes or drains. (setting-goals-that-work-with-your-adhd-brain)
- **Type-aligned and type-specific growth/conflict mapping** — Set goals matching natural tendencies and identify type-characteristic conflict triggers and matched coping strategies. (build-stronger-adhd-relationships-with-the-enneagram)
- **Cognitive-reframe anchors against internalized ableism** — Externalize a CBT-style reframe into physical/visual objects (wallpaper mantra, written "permission slip," recorded self-compassion message). (practical-guide-adhd-self-acceptance)
- **Reframe setbacks/mistakes as learning (growth mindset)** — Treat failures as learning opportunities and forward problem-solving ("What can I do better tomorrow?") rather than self-punishment. - **Self-compassion / friend-perspective reframing** — Counter shame by asking what you'd say to a friend in the same situation, then say it to yourself. (adhd-self-love, thriving-in-a-new-job-with-adhd, adhd-asking-for-help)
- **Mirror-traits reframing of self-talk** — Log negative self-thoughts for a week, then rewrite each into its positive complement ("stubborn" → "persistent"). (adhd-self-love)

- **Motivational-interviewing values redirection of impulses** — Reconnect discretionary spending/behavior to longer-term aspirational goals and what actually matters. (adhd-time-blindness-and-impulsive-spending, stop-adhd-impulse-buying)
- **Gratitude practice to shift mindset** — List things you're grateful for and recent wins to move from scarcity/anxiety toward abundance, reducing emotional pulls. (adhd-time-blindness-and-impulsive-spending)
- **Future-self visualization and gratitude** — Picture your future self as a real, separate person you are acting for, and verbalize gratitude to past/future selves. (adhd-building-sustaining-motivation, neurodiversity-health-magic-buttons)
- **Structured ADHD-story / narrative reframing prompts** — Guide the client to write/share their authentic ADHD story to shift from deficit-focused to strength-based self-narrative and build connection. (how-adhd-narratives-drive-change)
- **Translate self-understanding into environment and accommodation design** — Route reframed-question answers into concrete action: build suiting environments, request accommodations, add flexibility. (adhd-vs-me)
- **Assertive-communication scripts (broken record, emphatic escalation, "and" not "but")** — Hold your position respectfully and keep difficult feedback exchanges collaborative. (essential-workplace-skills-for-adhd)
- **Paraphrasing acknowledgment and clarifying questions** — Restate feedback to confirm alignment and ask open how/what questions to make vague feedback specific and actionable. (essential-workplace-skills-for-adhd)
- **Active listening / ask for clarification deliberately** — Stay focused on what's actually said and request detailed instructions rather than guessing. (thriving-in-a-new-job-with-adhd, adhd-mastering-job-offer-negotiations)
- **STAR+AV and Minto Pyramid answer structures** — Structure behavioral/interview answers as complete narratives or top-down (main point → support → summary) to compensate for tangential thinking. (adhd-interviewing-tips)

### W4 — ADHD + Depression / Comorbidity Coaching & Safety

- **Escalation / referral criteria with crisis resources** — Coach and client watch for red flags (symptoms persisting for weeks, deteriorating functioning, thoughts of self-harm) as triggers to consult a provider and access crisis support (988 / 741741). (adhd-and-anhedonia)

- **Screen for depression masked by ADHD/irritability** — Watch for depression presenting as irritability or worsening focus rather than classic sadness/withdrawal, since the conditions mask each other. (how-to-spot-depression-in-teens-with-adhd)
- **Map the ADHD–depression vicious cycle** — Identify with the client/family how ADHD-driven academic/social difficulties feed low mood, so interventions target the loop. (how-to-spot-depression-in-teens-with-adhd)
- **Coordinated treatment referral (therapy + medication)** — Recognize when to route the client to combined clinical care alongside coaching. (how-to-spot-depression-in-teens-with-adhd)
