# Verified Source Bibliography

> The anchor sources behind the validated doctrine, grouped by **evidence tier** and theme. This is a curated index for orientation and credibility — **full citations with DOIs/PMIDs/URLs live inline** in each stream file's **Sources** section (and the corrected-statistics trail is in `fact-checks.md`). When citing to a client or in writing, pull the exact reference from the relevant stream file, not from memory.

## How to read the tiers

| Tier | What it is | How much weight it carries |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1** | Consensus statements & clinical guidelines | Highest — cite confidently as the field's settled position. |
| **Tier 2** | Meta-analyses, systematic reviews, RCTs, large cohorts | Strong — primary empirical evidence; mind the stated caveats. |
| **Tier 3** | Authoritative expert/clinical works & bodies | Good for *framing and models* (Barkley, Brown, Solanto, MI); not a substitute for Tier 1–2 on prevalence/efficacy. |
| **Tier 4** | Coaching-standards bodies (self-published) | Establish that the *profession and its frameworks exist*; they say **nothing** about efficacy. |

A model or framework being only Tier 3 doesn't make it wrong — it makes it a *lens*, to be offered as such. Reserve "the evidence shows…" for Tier 1–2.

## Tier 1 — Consensus statements & clinical guidelines

- **Faraone SV, et al. (2021).** *The World Federation of ADHD International Consensus Statement: 208 Evidence-based Conclusions about the Disorder.* Neurosci Biobehav Rev 128:789–818. — The single most-used bedrock here: prevalence, persistence, neurobiology, mortality/suicide protective effects of treatment. *(Foundations, depression, habits, focus.)*
- **NICE NG87 — Attention deficit hyperactivity disorder: diagnosis and management.** — Under-recognition in girls/women; environmental modifications first for adults; structured psychological intervention/CBT. *(DSM/presentations, techniques, habits, toolkit.)*
- **Wolraich ML, et al. / AAP (2019).** *Clinical Practice Guideline for the Diagnosis, Evaluation, and Treatment of ADHD in Children and Adolescents.* Pediatrics. — Comorbidity is the norm; sequence/refer. *(Depression.)*
- **DSM-5-TR diagnostic criteria** (APA; criteria unchanged from DSM-5, reproduced via AAFP). — The diagnostic standard. Verbatim symptom wording is copyrighted — confirm against the manual before any published/clinical use. *(DSM/presentations, symptom inventory.)*

## Tier 2 — Meta-analyses, systematic reviews, RCTs, large cohorts

**Neurobiology & genetics**
- Faraone SV, Larsson H (2019). *Genetics of ADHD.* Mol Psychiatry 24:562–575. — Heritability mean ~74%.
- Mattheisen M, et al. (2022). *Shared & differentiating genetic architecture for ASD & ADHD.* Nat Genet 54:1470–1478. — Twin heritability 60–93% (≠ this paper's own SNP-heritability ~12.8%; see `fact-checks.md` #10).

**Symptoms & comorbidity**
- Choi W-S, et al. (2022). *Psychiatric comorbidities in adult ADHD vs non-ADHD: systematic review.* PLOS ONE 17(11):e0277175.
- Attoe DE, Climie EA (2023). *Miss. Diagnosis: A Systematic Review of ADHD in Adult Women.* J Atten Disord 27(7):645–657.
- Soler-Gutiérrez AM, et al. (2023). *Emotion dysregulation as a core symptom of adult ADHD: systematic review.* PLoS One.
- Shaw P, Stringaris A, Nigg J, Leibenluft E (2014). *Emotion Dysregulation in ADHD.* Am J Psychiatry 171(3):276–293. — Source of the "34–70%" range (see `fact-checks.md` #6).
- D'Agati E, et al. (2019). *Comorbidity between ADHD and anxiety across the lifespan.* Int J Psychiatry Clin Pract.
- Becker SP, et al. (2023). *Consensus change in terminology to Cognitive Disengagement Syndrome.* JAACAP.

**Depression & its treatment**
- Garcia-Argibay M, et al. (2024). *ADHD and MDD: Evidence From Multiple Genetically Informed Designs.* Biol Psychiatry 95(5):444–452. — ADHD→MDD, HR≈4.12 (sibling), MR causal.
- Wang S, et al. (2025). *Rates of Depression in Children and Adolescents With ADHD: meta-analysis.* J Atten Disord. — Pooled 11.3%; higher in females.
- Ekers D, et al. (2014). *Behavioural Activation for Depression: meta-analysis.* PLoS One 9(6):e100100. — SMD −0.74 vs control.
- Richards DA, et al. (2016). *COBRA: BA vs CBT non-inferiority RCT.* Lancet 388:871–880.
- Balán IC, et al. (2016). *Integrating MI and Brief Behavioral Activation.* Cogn Behav Pract 23(2):205–220.

**Lifespan & safety-critical risk**
- Dalsgaard S, et al. (2015). *Mortality in ADHD: nationwide cohort.* Lancet 385:2190–2196. — All-cause MRR 2.07; **accidents**-led (see `fact-checks.md` #4).
- Septier M, et al. (2019). *Suicidal spectrum behaviors and ADHD: meta-analysis (57 studies).* Neurosci Biobehav Rev 103:109–118. — Attempts OR 2.37; completed suicide OR 6.69. **Use this, not Dalsgaard, for suicide risk.**

**ADHD coaching outcome evidence**
- Field S, Parker D, Sawilowsky S, Rolands L (2010/2013). *Quantifying the Effectiveness of Coaching for College Students with ADHD* (Edge/Wayne State RCT). — LASSI total d≈1.02; **no GPA effect.**
- Prevatt F, Yelland S (2015). *An Empirical Evaluation of ADHD Coaching in College Students.* J Atten Disord 19(8):666–677. — Large pre/post gains; **no control group.**
- Ahmann E, Tuttle LJ, Saviet M, Wright SD (2018). *A Descriptive Review of ADHD Coaching Research.* JPED 31(1):17–39. — Best synthesis; only 2/19 studies were RCTs.

**CBT / executive-function programs**
- Solanto MV, et al. (2010). *Efficacy of Meta-Cognitive Therapy for Adult ADHD* (RCT, n=88). Am J Psychiatry 167(8):958–968. — Responder OR 5.41; the closest thing to an evidence-based EF curriculum.
- López-Pinar C, et al. / Cochrane. *Cognitive-behavioural interventions for ADHD in adults.* — Benefit vs waitlist; smaller vs active controls.

**Behavior-change techniques**
- Gollwitzer PM, Sheeran P (2006). *Implementation Intentions and Goal Achievement: meta-analysis (94 tests, n=8,461).* Adv Exp Soc Psychol 38:69–119. — **d≈0.65**; the strongest transferable tool.
- Lally P, et al. (2010). *How are habits formed.* Eur J Soc Psychol 40:998–1009. — Median **66 days**, range 18–254; "one miss doesn't matter."
- Gawrilow C, Gollwitzer PM (2008) / Gawrilow, Gollwitzer & Oettingen (2011). *If-then plans in children with ADHD.* — Normalised response inhibition / delay-of-gratification. (The direct ADHD signal for implementation intentions; in *children*.)
- Frost H, et al. (2018). *Effectiveness of MI: systematic review of reviews.* PLoS One 13(10):e0204890.
- Sibley MH, et al. (2021). *MI-Enhanced Behavior Therapy for Adolescents With ADHD (STAND): community RCT.* JAACAP 60(6):745–756. — MI improved **engagement/adherence**, not symptoms directly.
- Vermeulen-Oskam E, et al. (2024). *Current evidence of solution-focused brief therapy: meta-analysis.* Clin Psychol Rev 114:102512.
- Milkman KL, Minson JA, Volpp KGM (2014). *Temptation Bundling.* Manag Sci 60(2):283–299.
- Giné X, Karlan D, Zinman J (2010). *Commitment contract for smoking cessation (CARES).* Am Econ J Appl Econ 2(4):213–235. — Commitment-device evidence (non-ADHD).
- Beaton DM, Sirois F, Milne E (2022). *Self-compassion in the mental health of adults with ADHD.* J Clin Psychol 78(12):2497–2512.

**Symptom toolkit**
- Marx I, et al. (2021). *Altered Perceptual Timing in ADHD: meta-analysis (55 studies).* JAACAP.
- Zheng Q, et al. (2022). *Time Perception Deficits in Children/Adolescents with ADHD: meta-analysis.* J Atten Disord 26(2):267–281.
- Marx I, et al. (2021). *ADHD and the Choice of Small Immediate Over Larger Delayed Rewards: meta-analysis.* J Atten Disord 25(2):171–187.
- Martinussen R, et al. (2005). *Working Memory Impairments in Children With ADHD: meta-analysis.* JAACAP 44(4):377–384.
- Kasper LJ, Alderson RM, Hudec KL (2012). *Moderators of working memory deficits in ADHD: meta-analytic review.* Clin Psychol Rev 32(7):605–617.
- Gilbert SJ, et al. (2022). *Outsourcing Memory to External Tools: "Intention Offloading."* Psychon Bull Rev. — ~45%→~5% forgetting with reminders (general population).
- Wynchank D, et al. (2017). *Adult ADHD and Insomnia: update of the literature.* Curr Psychiatry Rep 19(12):98. — Insomnia 43–80% (see `fact-checks.md` #7).
- Fadeuilhe C, et al. (2021). *Insomnia Disorder in Adult ADHD.* Front Psychiatry 12:663889.
- Kidwell KM, et al. (2015). *Stimulant Medications and Sleep for Youth With ADHD: meta-analysis.* Pediatrics.
- Oliva F, et al. (2021). *Efficacy of mindfulness-based interventions in ADHD: meta-analysis.* J Affect Disord 292:475–486. — Complement, **not** replacement (see `fact-checks.md` #9).
- Eagle T, et al. (2023/2024). *Body Doubling with Neurodivergent Participants.* ACM ASSETS / TACCESS. — Self-report positive; controlled effect **not** established.

## Tier 3 — Authoritative expert/clinical works & bodies (framing & models)

- **Barkley RA (1997).** *Behavioral inhibition, sustained attention, and executive functions.* Psychol Bull 121(1):65–94. — The unifying EF/self-regulation theory.
- **Barkley RA.** *The Important Role of Executive Functioning and Self-Regulation in ADHD* (fact sheet, russellbarkley.org). — The four externalization principles, "point of performance," temporal myopia, "motivational prostheses."
- **Brown TE (2005/2008).** *Executive Functions: six aspects* (Yale Univ. Press / CHADD *Attention*). — The six-cluster model.
- **Arnsten AFT (2009).** *Neurobiology of ADHD: the prefrontal association cortex.* J Pediatr. — PFC + dopamine/norepinephrine inverted-U.
- **Solanto MV (2011).** *Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy for Adult ADHD: Targeting Executive Dysfunction.* Guilford. — The manualized 12-session program + its maxims.
- **Miller WR, Rollnick S (2023).** *Motivational Interviewing: Helping People Change and Grow* (4th ed.). Guilford. — PACE, OARS, change talk, the four processes.
- **Whitmore J.** *Coaching for Performance* (GROW: Goal-Reality-Options-Will; awareness + responsibility).
- **de Shazer S & Berg IK** — founders of Solution-Focused Brief Therapy (scaling, miracle question, exceptions).
- **Fogg BJ (2019).** *Tiny Habits* / Fogg Behavior Model (B=MAP).
- **Wood W (2019).** *Good Habits, Bad Habits* — context–repetition–reward; immediate reward; design over willpower.
- **Hallowell EM & Ratey JJ (2021).** *ADHD 2.0* — shame as "the single most debilitating part"; connection as #1 "treatment."
- **Brown B (2006).** *Shame Resilience Theory* — guilt ("I did something bad") vs. shame ("I am bad"); empathy as the antidote.
- **Mahan B & McCabe J (2024).** *Overcoming the Wall of Awful* — the emotional barrier to task initiation (community model, not peer-reviewed).
- **Hullinger AM & DiGirolamo JA / ICF (2018).** *Referring a Client to Therapy: A Set of Guidelines.* — The backbone of the W4.18 referral/scope protocol.
- **NIMH (2024).** *Brain Connections Associated With ADHD in Youth.*

## Tier 4 — Coaching-standards bodies (profession, not efficacy)

- **International Coaching Federation (ICF)** — Core Competencies (8/4 domains), Code of Ethics (§3.18, §3.23), Credentials (ACC/PCC/MCC).
- **Professional Association of ADHD Coaches (PAAC)** — Core Competencies / Five Essentials (ADHD Lens, Safety, Collaboration, Wonder, Action); Credentialing (CACP/PCAC); "complements clinical care, does not replace it."
- **ADD Coach Academy (ADDCA)** — ICF-accredited ADHD coach-training school.
- **ADHD Coaches Organization (ACO)** — professional membership body.
